Once you&apos;ve [set up Autopilot](/docs/agentic-ai/autopilot/setup), there are two ways to leverage it:

* **On-demand chat**: Ask ad hoc questions in the <DNT>AI</DNT> panel any time you want to investigate something. <DNT>Autopilot</DNT> is the default agent in the panel, so you no longer need to select it from a list before you start asking questions.
* **Automatic analysis on alerts**: Once you [connect Autopilot to a workflow](/docs/agentic-ai/autopilot/setup#alerts), its analysis is already waiting for you the moment you open a fired alert or issue—no need to ask anything.

This guide provides practical tips and best practices for getting the most out of <DNT>Autopilot</DNT>.

  To view recent conversations, click the clock icon in the upper left corner of the <DNT>AI</DNT> panel.
</Callout>

After you&apos;ve [set up Autopilot](/docs/agentic-ai/autopilot/setup), you can start using it to analyze your environment and investigate incidents. This guide provides practical tips and best practices for getting the most out of <DNT>Autopilot</DNT>.

## Consejos de uso [#usage-tips]

Siga estas mejores prácticas para obtener resultados óptimos de <DNT>Autopilot</DNT>:

**Consideraciones sobre el rango de tiempo**

<DNT>Autopilot</DNT> está diseñado para priorizar el análisis en vivo y normalmente busca de forma predeterminada eventos, problemas y entidades en las **últimas 24 horas**. Si desea analizar algo más antiguo, especifique una ventana de tiempo objetivo para el análisis.

**Optimización de consultas**

El agente utiliza herramientas diseñadas para convertir lenguaje natural en consultas NRQL, que luego puedes revisar y ejecutar en el generador de consultas. <DNT>Autopilot</DNT> está diseñado para analizar telemetry data y actualmente no está destinado ni diseñado para realizar revisiones o análisis de código fuente. Cuando haga referencia a conversaciones largas, limite el uso de pronombres ambiguos para ayudar a mejorar la precisión de los resultados.

**Revise siempre las sugerencias cuidadosamente**

Es posible que los agentes no siempre actúen como usted pretende. Por ejemplo, un agente puede hacer una sugerencia o iniciar un proceso que considere razonable, pero que puede no alinearse con lo que usted tenía en mente. Debido a esto, hemos incorporado un humano en el ciclo (usted) para la toma de ciertas decisiones en el diseño de <DNT>Autopilot</DNT> y proporcionamos controles configurables para el acceso y los permisos de <DNT>Autopilot</DNT> para tareas más automatizadas. Es importante que revise las sugerencias cuidadosamente y valide esas sugerencias y los próximos pasos para su propio contexto y circunstancias.

**Tiempo de respuesta**

Response times may vary depending on the scope and complexity of the investigation.

**Give feedback**

Use the thumbs-up and thumbs-down controls on any response, and add a comment. The most useful feedback states what you asked, what you expected, and what you received.

## Review Autopilot&apos;s reasoning [#reasoning]

While <DNT>Autopilot</DNT> is investigating, and after it responds, expand **Show reasoning** to audit what it did. This shows each step of the investigation, where information may have been pulled from, and whether the investigation step is still in progress. Completed steps will show a green checkmark. <DNT>Autopilot</DNT> flags steps that may have run into a problem, so you can spot where something went wrong.

## Conocimiento de New Relic AI con Autopilot [#knowledge]

You have the option to connect [<DNT>New Relic AI Knowledge</DNT>](/docs/agentic-ai/knowledge-integration/overview/) to <DNT>Autopilot</DNT> so that its answers take into context your team&apos;s own documents, such as runbooks or other process documents, in addition to <DNT>Autopilot</DNT>&apos;s general knowledge. Once connected, <DNT>Autopilot</DNT> will search your connected content during an investigation.

Con <DNT>New Relic AI Knowledge</DNT> habilitado, puede:

* Connect <DNT>Confluence</DNT> retros, postmortems, runbooks, and custom files so the agent&apos;s answers can strive to reflect your team&apos;s actual processes and practices.
* Search past incidents that may closely match what you&apos;re seeing now, the steps that fixed it, and the team that handled it—so you spend less time investigating from scratch.
* Ask questions in plain language, such as &quot;What are the standard triage steps for this alert?&quot; or &quot;Show me the runbook for a `database connection limit exceeded` error,&quot; and have content and context drawn directly from your own documentation.
* Type `/` in the <DNT>Autopilot</DNT> side panel to attach a specific knowledge document—a runbook, retro, or postmortem—to your prompt so the answer reflects that document.

For setup instructions and supported connectors, refer to [<DNT>New Relic AI Knowledge</DNT>](/docs/agentic-ai/knowledge-integration/overview/).

## Use Autopilot in Slack [#slack]

If an admin has connected your workspace to <DNT>Slack</DNT>, you can investigate without leaving the conversation. Mention the New Relic app (for example, `@New Relic`) in any channel where it&apos;s invited, and <DNT>Autopilot</DNT> replies in the same thread. You can:

* Ask why an alert triggered, check the health of a service, or analyze a recent deployment.
* Reply in an alert notification&apos;s thread to investigate it in context, without naming the entity.
* Ask follow-up questions in the same thread to dig deeper.

For workspace setup, identity linking, and the full usage guide, refer to [Autopilot Slack integration](/docs/agentic-ai/autopilot/connect-to-slack).

<Callout variant="important">
  Connected knowledge documents are visible to every user with <DNT>Autopilot</DNT> access in your organization, not just the person who connected them.
</Callout>

## Autopilot Memories [#memories]

If enabled, <DNT>Autopilot</DNT> Memory extracts operational knowledge from your interactions—past failure patterns, effective workarounds, and infrastructure quirks—and uses it as a persistent knowledge base <DNT>Autopilot</DNT> draws on in future investigations.

For details on how memory capture works as well as associated access permissions and data retention, see [Autopilot Memories](/docs/agentic-ai/autopilot/memories).
