Monitor the health and performance of your OpenTelemetry collectors with New Relic's collector observability feature. Get visibility into your collector's internal telemetry through a dedicated APM UI experience, helping you ensure your observability pipeline is running smoothly.

## What is collector observability? [#what-is]

OpenTelemetry collectors are critical components in your observability pipeline, responsible for receiving, processing, and exporting telemetry data. Just like any other service in your infrastructure, collectors themselves need monitoring to ensure they're performing optimally.

Collector observability leverages the collector's [internal telemetry](https://opentelemetry.io/docs/collector/internal-telemetry/) to provide insights into:

- **Collector health**: Is your collector running and responding?
- **Throughput metrics**: How much data is flowing through your collector?
- **Performance indicators**: Are there bottlenecks or processing delays?
- **Resource utilization**: How much CPU and memory is your collector consuming?
- **Component-level metrics**: How are individual receivers, processors, and exporters performing?

## Key capabilities [#capabilities]

### Dedicated APM UI experience

Your OpenTelemetry collectors appear as service entities in the APM & Services section of New Relic, providing you with:

- A familiar APM interface tailored for collector monitoring
- Summary dashboards with key performance indicators
- Infrastructure relationships showing the host or container running your collector
- Golden signals adapted for the collector's stream-processing nature

### Native internal telemetry

The collector emits its own telemetry about its operations:

- **Metrics**: Constant overhead metrics about all active components
- **Logs**: Sampled logs with minimal impact during normal operation
- **Traces**: Optional (experimental) traces for deep component-level insights

### Infrastructure relationships

When properly configured, collector observability can show infrastructure relationships:

- **Host-to-service**: Link collectors to the hosts they run on
- **Container-to-service**: Connect collectors to their container infrastructure
- Integration with existing infrastructure monitoring

## Why monitor your collectors? [#why-monitor]

Monitoring your OpenTelemetry collectors is essential for maintaining a healthy observability pipeline:

1. **Detect issues early**: Identify problems like memory leaks, CPU spikes, or data drops before they impact your downstream observability
2. **Optimize performance**: Understand bottlenecks and tune your collector configuration for better throughput
3. **Capacity planning**: Track resource utilization trends to plan for scaling
4. **Troubleshoot data gaps**: Quickly identify if missing telemetry is due to collector issues
5. **Validate configurations**: Ensure configuration changes have the desired effect on performance

## Get started [#get-started]

Ready to start monitoring your collectors? Follow our [setup guide](/docs/opentelemetry/collector-observability/collector-observability-setup) to:

1. Enable internal telemetry for your collector
2. Configure the collector to send telemetry to New Relic
3. Add the required entity tag to opt into the UI experience
4. View your collector in the APM UI

## Prerequisites [#prerequisites]

Before you begin, ensure you have:

- **NRDOT Collector version 1.10.0 or later**: This feature requires a recent collector version
- **New Relic account**: With a valid ingest license key
- **Configuration file access**: Ability to modify and deploy collector configurations
- **Basic OTel knowledge**: Familiarity with OpenTelemetry collector configuration

## Understanding costs [#costs]

Collector observability generates additional telemetry data that counts toward your data ingest. Review our guide on [understanding collector telemetry overhead](/docs/opentelemetry/collector-observability/collector-telemetry-overhead) to learn about:

- Billing during the preview period
- Data overhead from metrics, logs, and traces
- Cost optimization strategies

## Current limitations [#limitations]

As this feature is in public preview, please be aware of the following limitations:

- Collector telemetry schema is not yet stable and may change
- Only OTLP exporter format is supported (Prometheus format not supported)
- Some APM features are hidden as they don't apply to collectors (Apdex, traditional transactions)
- Custom metrics from contrib components have limited support

For a complete list of limitations, see our [setup guide](/docs/opentelemetry/collector-observability/collector-observability-setup#limitations).
