Collector observability generates additional telemetry data that counts toward your New Relic data ingest. This guide helps you understand the billing implications and how to optimize costs.

## Billing during preview [#billing-preview]

<Callout variant="important" title="Billing">
Your use of Collector Observability is billable during preview in accordance with your Order as applicable to the pricing model associated with your Account.

The costs associated with this feature are determined by the following factors, as applicable to the pricing model associated with your Account:

**Core Compute**: The Summary page, measured in Core CCU, is billable during preview. The Process page and HTTP/RPC page are not billable during preview.

**Data Ingest**: Additional data from the internal telemetry, measured in GB Ingested, is billable during preview.

If this feature becomes generally available, your use will be billable in accordance with your Order.
</Callout>

## Data overhead factors [#overhead-factors]

Like all telemetry, the collector's internal telemetry increases your data ingest. The overhead depends on your workload and configuration. Here are the key factors to consider:

### Metrics [#metrics]

**Impact**: Constant overhead, predictable costs

Metrics create constant overhead for the collector and all active components, regardless of throughput. Key characteristics:

- **Emission interval**: Metrics are emitted at a constant interval (60 seconds by default)
- **Scope**: Every active component (receivers, processors, exporters, extensions) emits metrics
- **Custom metrics**: Some components emit additional custom metrics beyond standard internal telemetry
- **Independence from workload**: Metric volume doesn't scale with data throughput

#### What generates metrics?

- **Core collector metrics**: Process uptime, runtime statistics, memory usage, CPU
- **Component metrics**: Per receiver, processor, and exporter metrics
- **Custom component metrics**: Additional metrics defined in component [metadata.yaml](https://github.com/open-telemetry/opentelemetry-collector-contrib/blob/main/processor/filterprocessor/metadata.yaml#L20)

#### Estimated overhead

For a typical collector with 3 receivers, 2 processors, and 2 exporters:
- Approximately 100-200 metric data points per minute
- Roughly 1-3 MB per day (uncompressed)

<Callout variant="tip">
**Cost optimization**: Metrics are the most predictable cost component. To reduce overhead:
- Set `INTERNAL_TELEMETRY_METRIC_LEVEL=normal` instead of `detailed`
- Increase metric emission interval (trade-off with granularity)
- Monitor your ingest to establish a baseline
</Callout>

### Logs [#logs]

**Impact**: Minimal during normal operation, higher during incidents

Logs have minimal impact during normal collector operation. Overhead characteristics:

- **Normal operation**: Very low volume with info-level logging
- **Error conditions**: Higher volume during errors, but mitigated by sampling
- **Sampling algorithm**: Allows a constant number of logs per defined interval, then samples at a static rate
- **Configurable**: Fully customizable via `service::telemetry::logs::sampling`

#### When do logs increase?

- Configuration errors or validation issues
- Authentication failures
- Network connectivity problems
- Component-level errors (e.g., exporter retries)
- Resource exhaustion warnings

#### Log sampling configuration

By default, logs are sampled to prevent cost spikes during error conditions:

```yaml
service:
  telemetry:
    logs:
      level: info
      sampling:
        enabled: true
        initial: 10      # Always allow first 10 logs per interval
        thereafter: 100  # Then sample 1 in every 100 logs
        tick: 10s        # Reset interval
```

#### Estimated overhead

- **Normal operation**: &lt; 1 MB per day
- **Error spike**: 5-10 MB per day (with sampling)
- **Without sampling**: Could be significantly higher during incidents

<Callout variant="tip">
**Cost optimization**:
- Keep log sampling enabled (default)
- Use `info` or `warn` level for production
- Only use `debug` level temporarily for troubleshooting
- Configure `INTERNAL_TELEMETRY_LOG_LEVEL=warn` to reduce normal operation logs
</Callout>

### Traces [#traces]

**Impact**: Unpredictable, potentially high overhead

Traces are **disabled by default** and considered experimental for collector observability.

#### Why traces are disabled

1. **Limited maturity**: Not all components are instrumented yet
   - See [GitHub issue #8804](https://github.com/open-telemetry/opentelemetry-collector/issues/8804) for instrumentation status
   - Processors are particularly under-instrumented as of v0.135.0

2. **Unpredictable overhead**: Trace volume varies significantly with:
   - Your data throughput
   - Batching configuration from upstream agents
   - Number of spans being processed
   - Pipeline complexity

3. **No adaptive sampling**: Without adaptive sampling, it's impossible to provide a universal recommendation that works for all use cases

4. **Cost risk**: Enabling traces without careful monitoring could lead to unexpected data ingest costs

#### When traces become valuable

Once instrumentation matures, traces will provide valuable insights:
- Time spent processing data at the component level
- Bottleneck identification in complex pipelines
- End-to-end request flow through the collector

#### Experimental trace enablement

If you want to experiment with traces as they develop:

```bash
INTERNAL_TELEMETRY_TRACE_LEVEL=info
INTERNAL_TELEMETRY_TRACE_SAMPLE_RATIO=0.001  # 0.1% sampling
```

**Important guidelines**:
1. Start with a very low sampling ratio (0.001 or 0.1%)
2. Monitor your data ingest closely for several days
3. Gradually increase if needed and costs are acceptable
4. Use a separate license key to isolate trace costs
5. Be prepared to disable if costs spike

#### Estimated overhead

Highly variable based on workload:
- **Low throughput** (&lt; 1000 spans/sec): 10-50 MB per day at 0.1% sampling
- **Medium throughput** (1000-10000 spans/sec): 50-500 MB per day at 0.1% sampling
- **High throughput** (&gt; 10000 spans/sec): &gt; 500 MB per day at 0.1% sampling

<Callout variant="caution">
**We strongly recommend keeping traces disabled** unless you have a specific need and can monitor costs closely. Wait for trace instrumentation to mature before enabling in production.
</Callout>

## Separating collector observability costs [#separate-costs]

To track collector observability costs separately from your regular telemetry:

### Use a different license key

Configure a separate ingest license key for internal telemetry:

```bash
INTERNAL_TELEMETRY_NEW_RELIC_LICENSE_KEY='your-separate-license-key'
NEW_RELIC_LICENSE_KEY='your-regular-data-license-key'
```

### Query by instrumentation provider

Filter for collector telemetry in your queries:

```sql
FROM Metric, Log, Span
SELECT bytecountestimate()
WHERE instrumentation.provider = 'opentelemetry'
AND service.name LIKE '%collector%'
SINCE 1 day ago
```

### Use data ingest dashboards

Create a custom dashboard to monitor collector observability ingest:

```sql
-- Metric ingest from collectors
FROM Metric
SELECT bytecountestimate() / 1e9 AS 'GB Ingested'
WHERE instrumentation.provider = 'opentelemetry'
AND newrelic.service.type = 'otel_collector'
FACET service.name
SINCE 1 day ago
TIMESERIES

-- Log ingest from collectors
FROM Log
SELECT bytecountestimate() / 1e9 AS 'GB Ingested'
WHERE instrumentation.provider = 'opentelemetry'
AND newrelic.service.type = 'otel_collector'
FACET service.name
SINCE 1 day ago
TIMESERIES
```

## Cost optimization strategies [#optimization]

### 1. Start with defaults

Begin with the default configuration to establish a baseline:
- Detailed metrics
- Info-level logs with sampling
- Traces disabled

Monitor for one week to understand your typical overhead.

### 2. Adjust metric detail level

If metric costs are higher than expected:

```bash
INTERNAL_TELEMETRY_METRIC_LEVEL=normal  # Reduce from 'detailed'
```

This reduces the number of metric dimensions and data points.

### 3. Increase log threshold

For stable production collectors:

```bash
INTERNAL_TELEMETRY_LOG_LEVEL=warn  # Only warnings and errors
```

### 4. Tune log sampling

For high-volume error scenarios:

```yaml
service:
  telemetry:
    logs:
      sampling:
        initial: 5        # Reduce from 10
        thereafter: 200   # Increase sampling ratio from 100
```

### 5. Monitor multiple collectors

If you have many collectors, consider:
- Enabling full observability on critical collectors only
- Using reduced detail levels on less critical collectors
- Rotating detailed monitoring across collector groups

### 6. Use sampling for experimentation

When testing new configurations:
- Enable detailed telemetry temporarily
- Collect data for analysis
- Reduce detail level once validated

## Estimating your costs [#estimating-costs]

To estimate collector observability costs for your environment:

### Step 1: Identify your collectors

Count the number of collectors you plan to monitor:
- Gateway collectors
- Host collectors
- Sidecar collectors

### Step 2: Estimate per-collector overhead

Based on default configuration (metrics + logs, no traces):

| Collector type | Components | Estimated daily data | Estimated monthly data |
|---------------|------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Simple (3-5 components) | Few receivers/exporters | 2-5 MB | 60-150 MB |
| Medium (6-10 components) | Multiple pipelines | 5-10 MB | 150-300 MB |
| Complex (10+ components) | Many processors/exporters | 10-20 MB | 300-600 MB |

### Step 3: Calculate total overhead

```
Total monthly data = (Number of collectors) × (Per-collector data)
```

Example: 10 medium collectors = 10 × 200 MB = 2 GB/month

### Step 4: Convert to cost

Apply your New Relic data ingest pricing:
- Check your contract for GB pricing
- Multiply by estimated monthly data
- Add Core CCU costs for Summary page usage

<Callout variant="tip">
These are estimates. Actual overhead varies based on:
- Collector activity and error rates
- Configuration detail levels
- Number of active components
- Whether traces are enabled
</Callout>

## Monitoring your overhead [#monitoring-overhead]

Set up alerts to monitor collector observability costs:

### Data ingest spike alert

```sql
FROM Metric
SELECT bytecountestimate() / 1e9 AS 'GB Ingested'
WHERE instrumentation.provider = 'opentelemetry'
AND newrelic.service.type = 'otel_collector'
SINCE 1 hour ago
```

Alert when hourly ingest exceeds your expected baseline by 50%.

### Daily cost tracking

Create a dashboard to track daily trends:

```sql
FROM Metric, Log
SELECT bytecountestimate() / 1e9 AS 'Total GB'
WHERE newrelic.service.type = 'otel_collector'
FACET service.name
SINCE 7 days ago
TIMESERIES 1 day
```

Review weekly to identify unexpected increases.
