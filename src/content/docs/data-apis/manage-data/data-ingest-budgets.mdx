Data ingest budgets help you control costs by setting monthly data limits (GB), tracking usage in real-time, and alerting you before you exceed your budget.

**Key capabilities:**

- Set budgets at organization and account levels
- Track data ingestion against your limits in real-time
- Receive alerts at customizable thresholds (for example, 75%, 90%, 95%)
- Prevent budget overages before they occur

## Prerequisites

To create, edit, or delete data ingest budgets, you need:

- Organization manager role
- Paid data ingest subscription
- Single-tenant organization

<Callout variant="tip">
  Multi-tenant organizations cannot use this feature. All users with account access can view budgets and receive notifications if configured as destinations.
</Callout>

## Set up organization-level budgets

Organization budgets track total data ingestion across all accounts.

<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Access data budgets

    1. Go to **[one.newrelic.com](https://one.newrelic.com/)** > **[user menu](/docs/accounts/accounts-billing/general-account-settings/intro-account-settings)** > **Administration** > **Data Management** > **Data Budgets**
    2. If you have a contract commitment, your organization budget appears automatically. Otherwise, click **Create organization budget**

    <Callout variant="tip">
      Default organization budgets are based on your monthly contract commitments but don't include notifications. You must configure thresholds and destinations to receive alerts.
    </Callout>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Set monthly limit

    1. Click **Manage limits** in the Organization Budget section
    2. In the **Set monthly budget limits** section, enter your monthly data limit in GB

    <Callout variant="important">
      By default, this is configured based on your monthly contract commitments. You can adjust the amount based on your expected usage.
    </Callout>

    3. Click **Next**
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Configure notifications

    1. In the **Thresholds** section, click **+ Add a threshold** and enter the percentage at which you want to receive notifications (for example, 75%, 90%, or 95%)
    2. In the **Destinations** section, add at least one destination to be notified:
       - **Email**: Enter the email addresses of New Relic users to notify about the budget
       - **Webhook**: Enter the webhook URL to send notifications to a specific endpoint

    <Callout variant="important">
      You must add at least one threshold and one destination to save your budget.
    </Callout>

    3. Click **Finish**
  </Step>
</Steps>

Your organization budget will now track your data ingestion usage and notify you when you reach the defined thresholds.

## Set up account-level budgets

Account budgets allocate portions of your organization budget to specific accounts (teams, applications, or business units).

<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Create an account-level budget

    1. From the **Data Budgets** page, click **+ Add functional budget**
    2. The **Create account level budget** page appears
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Configure budget details

    1. In the **Budget name** field, enter a descriptive name for your budget
    2. In the **Select accounts** section, choose up to 5 accounts that don't already have budgets assigned

    <Callout variant="tip">
      Accounts that already have budgets will appear disabled in the selection list.
    </Callout>

    3. Click **Next**
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Allocate data budget

    1. On the **Allocate budget** page, you'll see how many GB are remaining in your organization budget
    2. Enter the amount of GB you want to allocate to this account-level budget

    <Callout variant="tip">
      You can allocate more than your organization budget. This allows flexible planning but may result in overages.
    </Callout>

    3. Click **Next**
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Set up account-level notifications

    1. In the **Thresholds** section, click **+ Add a threshold** and enter the percentage at which you want to receive notifications for this account-level budget
    2. In the **Destinations** section, configure where notifications should be sent:
       - **Email**: Enter the email addresses of New Relic users to notify about the budget
       - **Webhook**: Enter the webhook URL to send notifications to a specific endpoint

    <Callout variant="important">
      You must add at least one threshold and one destination to save your budget.
    </Callout>

    3. Click **Finish**
  </Step>
</Steps>

Your account-level budget is now created and will appear in the budgets list.

## View and manage existing budgets

Once you've created account-level budgets, you can view and manage them from the **Data Budgets** page.

### Budget information displayed

For each account-level budget, you'll see:

- **Budget name**: The name you assigned to the budget
- **Status**: Current status of the budget
- **GB allocation**: How much data (GB) has been allocated to this budget
- **GB utilized**: How much of the allocated data has been used
- **Utilization percentage**: Percentage of the budget that has been used

### Manage existing budgets

To manage an existing account-level budget:

1. Locate the budget in the list and click the three-dot menu
2. Choose from the following options:
    - **View details**: See detailed information about the budget, including utilization graphs, thresholds, notification settings, associated accounts, and data sources
    - **Edit**: Modify the budget allocation, thresholds, or notification settings
    - **Delete**: Remove the budget

### View budget details

When you click on a budget name or select **View details**, you'll see a detailed page showing:

- **Total allocation**: The total GB allocated to this budget
- **Utilization**: How much has been utilized with a visual graph showing usage over time
- **Thresholds**: The notification thresholds you've configured
- **Notification destinations**: Where alerts are sent when thresholds are exceeded
- **Associated accounts**: The accounts included in this budget
- **Data sources**: Information about the data sources contributing to the budget usage

## Best practices

- **Set realistic limits**: Base budgets on historical usage and expected growth
- **Use multiple thresholds**: Set alerts at 75%, 90%, and 95% to allow time for action
- **Allocate by team or project**: Use account-level budgets to track high-usage areas
- **Review monthly**: Adjust budgets when usage patterns or monitoring capabilities change
- **Analyze trends**: Use utilization graphs to identify spikes or anomalies
