You create, update, and delete <DNT>cross account destinations</DNT> exclusively through <DNT>NerdGraph</DNT>. There's no UI for these actions. Once a <DNT>cross account destination</DNT> exists, however, it's visible and reusable anywhere in your organization's UI, so you don't have to recreate the same destination in every account.

For example, if your organization has hundreds of accounts and you want all of them to send alerts to the same <DNT>PagerDuty</DNT> or <DNT>Slack</DNT> destination, you create the destination once, at the organization level, and reuse it in workflows across every account—no need to repeat the setup account by account.

There are two places you'll encounter <DNT>cross account destinations</DNT> in the UI: the <DNT>Destinations</DNT> page, and the channel step of a workflow.

## View destinations on the Destinations page [#view-destinations-page]

1. Go to <DNT>**[one.newrelic.com](https://one.newrelic.com) > Alerts > Destinations**</DNT>.
2. Use the <DNT>**Destination scope**</DNT> filter to narrow the list:
   - **Account scope**: destinations available only in the current account.
   - **Cross account scope**: destinations available across every account in your organization.
3. Tell the two scopes apart by their icon:
   - An account icon, with the account name and ID, marks an account-scope destination.
   - An organization icon, with a note that all accounts in your organization can use the destination, marks a <DNT>cross account destination</DNT>.

<Callout variant="important">
You can edit, delete, or test only account-scope destinations from this page. For <DNT>cross account destinations</DNT>, the **Edit** and **Delete** options don't work. To update, delete, or test a <DNT>cross account destination</DNT>, use the [NerdGraph mutations](/docs/apis/nerdgraph/examples/cross-account-destinations/manage-cross-account-destinations) described on the previous page.
</Callout>

## Use a destination when creating a workflow channel [#use-destination-workflow]

A workflow sends notifications through one or more channels, and each channel uses a destination (the underlying integration, such as a <DNT>Slack</DNT> workspace or email address). When you add a channel to a workflow, both account-scope and <DNT>cross account destinations</DNT> appear together in the destination list, for any destination type—email, <DNT>Slack</DNT>, <DNT>Jira</DNT>, <DNT>ServiceNow</DNT>, webhook, <DNT>PagerDuty</DNT>, and so on.

1. Go to <DNT>**one.newrelic.com > Alerts > Workflows**</DNT> and select or create a workflow.
2. Add the filter criteria that determines when the workflow triggers (for example, issues with a specific priority).
3. Add a channel to the workflow, and select a destination type.
4. Select the destination to use from the destination list. You can tell account-scope and <DNT>cross account destinations</DNT> apart the same way as on the <DNT>Destinations</DNT> page.
5. Configure the channel-specific details (for example, an email subject and custom details, or a target <DNT>Slack</DNT> channel), and save the workflow.
