Once you've [set up Autopilot](/docs/agentic-ai/autopilot/setup), there are two ways to leverage it:

* **On-demand chat**: Ask ad hoc questions in the <DNT>AI</DNT> panel any time you want to investigate something. <DNT>Autopilot</DNT> is the default agent in the panel, so you no longer need to select it from a list before you start asking questions.
* **Automatic analysis on alerts**: Once you [connect Autopilot to a workflow](/docs/agentic-ai/autopilot/setup#alerts), its analysis is already waiting for you the moment you open a fired alert or issue—no need to ask anything.

This guide provides practical tips and best practices for getting the most out of <DNT>Autopilot</DNT>.

<Callout variant="tip">
  To view recent conversations, click the clock icon in the upper left corner of the <DNT>AI</DNT> panel.
</Callout>

## Usage tips [#usage-tips]

Follow these best practices to get optimal results from <DNT>Autopilot</DNT>:

**Time range considerations**

<DNT>Autopilot</DNT> is designed to prioritize live analysis and typically defaults to searching for events, issues, and entities within the **last 24 hours**. If you want to analyze something older, specify a targeted time window for analysis.

**Query optimization**

The agent uses tools designed to convert natural language into NRQL queries, which you can then review and run in the query builder. <DNT>Autopilot</DNT> is designed to analyze telemetry data and is not currently intended or designed to perform source code review or source code analysis. When referencing long conversations, limit ambiguous pronoun usage to help improve the accuracy of the results.

**Always review suggestions carefully**

Agents may not always act as you intend. For example, an agent may make a suggestion or initiate a process that it deems reasonable, but that may not align with what you had in mind. Due to this, we have incorporated a human-in-the-loop (you) for certain decision making in the <DNT>Autopilot</DNT> design and provide configurable controls for <DNT>Autopilot</DNT> access and permissions for more automated tasks. It is important for you to review suggestions carefully and validate those suggestions and next steps for your own context and circumstances.

**Response time**

On-demand chat investigations typically take 30 to 90 seconds. Very large investigations can take five minutes or more.

**Give feedback**

Use the thumbs-up and thumbs-down controls on any response, and add a comment. The most useful feedback states what you asked, what you expected, and what you received.

## Review Autopilot's reasoning [#reasoning]

While <DNT>Autopilot</DNT> is investigating, and after it responds, expand **Show reasoning** to audit what it did. This shows each step of the investigation, where it pulled information from, and whether it's still in progress. Completed steps get a green checkmark. <DNT>Autopilot</DNT> flags any step that ran into a problem, so you can spot where something went wrong.

## New Relic AI Knowledge with Autopilot [#knowledge]

Connect [<DNT>New Relic AI Knowledge</DNT>](/docs/agentic-ai/knowledge-integration/overview/) to <DNT>Autopilot</DNT> so its answers draw on your team's own documents, not just general knowledge. Once connected, the agent searches your connected content during every investigation.

With <DNT>New Relic AI Knowledge</DNT> enabled, you can:

- Connect <DNT>Confluence</DNT> retros, postmortems, runbooks, and custom files (PDFs, CSVs, text files) so the agent's answers reflect your team's actual practices.
- Find the past incident that matches what you're seeing now, the steps that fixed it, and the team that handled it—so you spend less time investigating from scratch.
- Ask questions in plain language, such as "What are the standard triage steps for this alert?" or "Show me the runbook for a `database connection limit exceeded` error," and get an answer drawn directly from your own documentation.
- Schedule regular content updates so the agent always has access to your most current documents.
- Type `/` in the <DNT>Autopilot</DNT> side panel to attach a specific knowledge document—a runbook, retro, or postmortem—to your prompt so the answer reflects that document.

For setup instructions and supported connectors, see [<DNT>New Relic AI Knowledge</DNT>](/docs/agentic-ai/knowledge-integration/overview/).

<Callout variant="tip">
  Connected knowledge documents are visible to every user with <DNT>Autopilot</DNT> access in your organization, not just the person who connected them.
</Callout>
