The <DNT>New Relic SRE Agent</DNT> is an intelligent on-call teammate that integrates directly with your telemetry data and established workflows to transform incident management. By automating data correlation and diagnosis, it moves beyond simple alerting to provide active investigation, diagnosis, and remediation.

<Callout variant="caution">
  **Compliance restrictions:** Due to security and compliance requirements, you must not use the <DNT>New Relic SRE Agent</DNT> if your accounts or data fall under FedRAMP or HIPAA compliance mandates. Use of this public preview feature with FedRAMP or HIPAA-regulated accounts is strictly prohibited.
</Callout>

## Key capabilities

Here are some examples of how the <DNT>SRE Agent</DNT> supports your operations:

* **Alert triage and validation**: The agent acts as a first-line filter for incoming notifications. It evaluates the context of an alert against historical baselines and current system health to determine if an event is a high-priority anomaly or an expected fluctuation, reducing alert fatigue for on-call engineers.

* **Real-time entity health**: The agent provides an instant, high-level pulse check on any part of your stack. By correlating golden signal metrics with underlying infrastructure performance, it delivers a narrative summary of health that goes deeper than a simple green or red status.

* **Change intelligence**: Deployments are often the catalyst for instability. The <DNT>SRE Agent</DNT> reviews deployment markers and compares pre and post change telemetry to identify performance regressions, new error patterns, or latency spikes introduced by recent updates.

* **Deep incident analysis**: When system degradation occurs, the agent performs automated root-cause discovery. It traverses distributed traces, analyzes log patterns, and inspects resource metrics to help identify potential technical failures or downstream dependencies that may be contributing to an outage.

* **Automated remediation**: Beyond diagnosis, the <DNT>SRE Agent</DNT> suggests specific technical interventions-such as rolling back a problematic deployment or adjusting resource allocations.

## How the SRE Agent analyzes your environment [#how-it-works]

The <DNT>SRE Agent</DNT> acts as an intelligent layer between your complex system data and actionable insights. When you ask the agent a question, it follows three primary analysis pillars:

* **Comprehensive context gathering**: The agent begins by mapping your architecture. It identifies relevant entities (services, hosts, or containers), explores their relationships and dependencies, and checks for recent change events or deployments that might have triggered a shift in performance.

* **Deep telemetry investigation**: Once context is established, the agent dives into the data:
  * **Metrics and trends**: Analyzes throughput, latency, and error rates while forecasting future system behavior.
  * **Logs and errors**: Analyzes distributed traces, error groups, and log patterns to isolate the location and cause of an anomaly.
  * **Health and alerts**: Reviews active incidents, alert policies, and security vulnerabilities to understand the current blast radius.

* **Automated root cause and impact analysis**: The agent performs causal analysis—correlating performance dips with specific transaction patterns, infrastructure bottlenecks (such as Kafka lag), and end-user impact. It can also convert natural language into complex NRQL queries to surface data points that manual investigation might miss.
