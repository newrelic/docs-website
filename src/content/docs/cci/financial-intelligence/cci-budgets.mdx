A budget sets a spending limit for your account over one to twelve months. You can track actual spending against this limit to control cloud costs.

## Create a budget [#create-budget]

1. Go to <DNT>[**one.newrelic.com**](https://one.newrelic.com)</DNT> > <DNT>**All Capabilities**</DNT> > <DNT>**Cloud Cost Intelligence**</DNT> > <DNT>**Budgets**</DNT>.
2. Click <DNT>**+ Create a budget**</DNT> on the budgets page.
3. Enter a name for your budget.
4. Choose your setup type:
   - <DNT>**Basic Setup**</DNT>: Quick budget creation with essential fields (budget amount, type, scope, alerts)
   - <DNT>**Advanced Setup**</DNT>: Additional options including custom time duration and multiple scope configurations

## Monitor budgets

After creating budgets, the budgets page displays key metrics and an overview table to track spending.

### Budget summary

The Summary section at the top of the budgets page displays key metrics:
- <DNT>**Total**</DNT>: Total number of budgets created
- <DNT>**Alerts Configured**</DNT>: Number of budgets with alert policies set up
- <DNT>**Under Budget**</DNT>: Number of budgets with spending below the threshold
- <DNT>**At Risk**</DNT>: Number of budgets approaching their spending limit
- <DNT>**Breached**</DNT>: Number of budgets that have exceeded their spending limit

### Budget overview

The Overview section includes filter options to view budgets by status:
  - <DNT>**Under Budget (&lt; 50%)**</DNT>: Budgets with spending below 50% of the allocated amount
  - <DNT>**At Risk (50% - 100%)**</DNT>: Budgets with spending between 50% and 100% of the allocated amount
  - <DNT>**Breached (&gt; 100%)**</DNT>: Budgets that have exceeded their allocated amount

## View budget details

Click the ellipsis icon (...) next to a budget in the overview table and select <DNT>**Show details**</DNT> to view detailed information about your budget.

### Configuration details

The left panel displays your budget configuration:

- <DNT>**Name**</DNT>: The budget identifier
- <DNT>**Created by**</DNT>: User who created the budget
- <DNT>**Budget Allocation**</DNT>: Total amount allocated for the budget period
- <DNT>**Budget Consumption**</DNT>: Current spending amount
- <DNT>**Time range**</DNT>: Start and end dates for the budget period
- <DNT>**Type**</DNT>: Cost category being tracked
- <DNT>**Scope**</DNT>: Accounts or resources covered (for example, Account Id)

### Budget breakdown table

The main section displays a table with monthly budget details:

- <DNT>**Start date**</DNT>: Beginning of each month in the budget period
- <DNT>**End date**</DNT>: End of each month in the budget period
- <DNT>**Budget Allocation**</DNT>: Amount allocated for that month
- <DNT>**Budget Consumption**</DNT>: Actual spending for that month

### Budget allocation chart

Below the table, a visualization displays:
- Monthly budget allocations as bar charts
- Time-based view showing spending patterns across the budget period
- Color-coded bars to track budget consumption trends

### Alert Policies

The <DNT>**Alert Policies**</DNT> section at the bottom shows:
- <DNT>**Set up alerts**</DNT> toggle to enable alerts that help you stay on top of usage and meet your budget goals. When enabled, you can:
  - Select an alert threshold percentage (100%, 80%, 70%, or 60% of budget)
  - Choose to use an existing policy or create a new policy
  - Select or create an alert policy to receive notifications

  <Callout variant="tip">
    Use the monthly breakdown table to identify specific months where spending patterns deviate from your budget. This helps you adjust spending or reallocate budget amounts before exceeding your limits.
  </Callout>
