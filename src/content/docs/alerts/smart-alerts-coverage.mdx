With [smart alerts](/docs/alerts/smart-alerts), you can find where your entities lack alert coverage and close the gaps by applying recommended standards.


<Callout variant="tip">
Smart alerts are account-scoped. You can't view or manage alert coverage across multiple accounts from a single view.
</Callout>

In the New Relic UI, select <DNT>**Alerts** > **Alerts Overview**</DNT>.

Smart alerts show how your entities measure up to recommended standards, whether you're new to alerting or already have conditions in place. New Relic recommends the standard conditions for each entity type, so you don't start from scratch. Smart alerts doesn't create conditions on your behalf — it surfaces which gaps exist so you can create entity-scoped conditions to close them:

<Steps>
  <Step>
    ## Analyze coverage gaps [#analyze-coverage]

    Start on the <DNT>**Alerts Overview**</DNT> page, where you can check how your entities measure up to recommended standards, which entities have no condition, and which conditions are noisy or produce no signal. Act on what you find:

    * To close coverage gaps, continue with the steps below.
    * To quiet conditions that alert too often, refer to [Reduce noise](/docs/alerts/smart-alerts-quality#reduce-noise).
    * To fix conditions that stopped reporting, refer to [Manage conditions without signals](/docs/alerts/smart-alerts-quality#no-signals).

    You may notice differences in the coverage numbers:

    * The totals in <DNT>**Entities without alert conditions**</DNT> and <DNT>**Standards coverage for your entities**</DNT> may differ — Standards coverage counts only supported entity types: services (APM), browser applications, mobile applications, synthetic monitors, and hosts.
    * The Hosts entity type doesn't show coverage percentages — because host entities are added and removed frequently.
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ## Check standards coverage [#check-standards-coverage]

    To review coverage for every entity type, on the <DNT>**Alerts Overview**</DNT> page, click <DNT>**Manage standards coverage**</DNT>. This opens the <DNT>**Coverage status**</DNT> page.

    Expand each entity type to review the condition standards New Relic recommends for it, and how many of your entities meet each standard. From here, you can do the following:

    * **Inspect the entities under a standard**: Click <DNT>**Standard coverage**</DNT> bars, or hyperlinked entity number, and filter the list by <DNT>**Covered**</DNT>, <DNT>**Partially covered**</DNT>, or <DNT>**Not covered**</DNT>.
    * **Proceed to close the gaps for a standard**: Click <DNT>**Configure**</DNT> if the standard isn't covered, or <DNT>**Manage**</DNT> if it's partially or fully covered. Either opens the standard's <DNT>**Edit conditions**</DNT> page.

    <Callout variant="tip">
    Smart alerts currently don't have recommendations for entities unique to your applications or business. You can get those from <DNT>**Others Under Consideration**</DNT> and [create your alert conditions manually](/docs/alerts/create-alert/create-alert-condition/alert-conditions/#create-alert-condition).
    </Callout>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ## Edit or create conditions [#edit-conditions]

    The <DNT>**Edit conditions**</DNT> page lists every condition for that standard, with each condition's threshold type, the number of entities it's active for, and its status. From here, you can do the following:

    * **Disable a condition**: Turn off its toggle, or open it with <DNT>**Edit**</DNT> and disable it there.
    * **Delete a condition**: Click <DNT>**Edit**</DNT> for the condition, then delete it from the condition's page.
    * **Adjust thresholds for an existing condition**: Click <DNT>**Edit**</DNT> for the condition and use the guided flow to update its thresholds.
    * **Add conditions for entities that aren't covered**: Select <DNT>**Create new alert conditions**</DNT> and configure the new condition.

    ### Legacy conditions vs. new smart alert conditions [#legacy-vs-smart]

    You can edit a legacy condition to include other entities or create a new smart alert condition for uncovered entities. New Relic recommends creating a new condition — smart alerts sets a recommended threshold per entity based on its signal history. Editing a legacy condition and adding entities applies a single threshold to all selected entities, regardless of their individual behavior.

    As smart alert conditions are entity-scoped, a new condition covers all entities matching the query — including those already covered by the legacy condition. To avoid duplicate conditions for those entities, use filters to exclude them when configuring the new condition. Refer to [Configure new alert condition](#configure-condition) for details.
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ## Configure new alert condition [#configure-condition]

    The <DNT>**Create an alert condition**</DNT> page pre-scopes the condition to all entities of that standard's type. A recommended threshold is set for each entity based on its signal history. Before saving, you can do the following:

    * **Refine the query**: Add filters to narrow the condition to a specific signal or subset of entities. Recommended thresholds update to reflect the updated query's signal history.
    * **Add a title**: Set a name that applies to all alert events this condition generates.
    * **Set the issue creation preference**: Choose how New Relic groups alert events from this condition into issues:
      * **One issue per policy**: Groups all alert events from any condition in this policy into a single issue. Use this when your policy monitors a focused scope and you want the fewest notifications.
      * **One issue per condition**: Creates a separate issue for each condition. All signals that breach the same condition are added to that condition's issue. Use this when you want to track incidents by condition.
      * **One issue per condition and signal**: Creates a separate issue for each unique condition-and-entity combination. This is the most granular option and produces the most notifications.

    <Callout variant="caution">
    The new condition targets all entities matching the query — not only those without existing coverage. If some entities are already covered by other conditions, this results in duplicate conditions for them. To avoid duplicates, refine the query to filter out already-covered entities before saving.
    </Callout>

    To adjust the recommended thresholds for the entities this condition covers, refer to [Adjust recommended thresholds](#adjust-thresholds).
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Adjust recommended thresholds [#adjust-thresholds]

When you create or edit a standard's conditions, New Relic sets a recommended threshold for each entity based on its signal history. You can adjust thresholds for all entities at once using the noise slider, or tune them individually. For conditions covering more than 300 entities, use [Threshold scope](#threshold-scope).

Noise describes how sensitive a threshold is: A noisier threshold triggers alerts more often and increases the risk of false positives, while a less noisy threshold triggers fewer alerts but may miss some real issues.

**Adjust all thresholds at once**

Drag the noise slider on the condition page. Each entity's threshold is scaled by a multiplicative factor:

* **Less noisy**: Tightens thresholds, reducing how often they trigger.
* **More noisy**: Loosens thresholds, increasing how often they trigger.

To prevent any entity's threshold from going outside an acceptable range, set a minimum value, a maximum value, or both on the condition page. New Relic keeps the threshold within those limits regardless of the slider position.

**Adjust a single entity's threshold**

To set the threshold for a single entity, do one of the following:

* Enter a value directly in the threshold field.
* Turn on <DNT>**Minimize noise**</DNT> to apply the lowest-noise threshold automatically.
* Click <DNT>**Fine-tune**</DNT> to open a chart of that entity's signal over the past three days with the current threshold shown as a reference. Adjust the value against the trend and save it.

Any entity whose threshold you set manually is tagged as <DNT>**Threshold set by user**</DNT>, to distinguish it from a recommendation. You can revert to the recommended value at any time.

### Threshold scope [#threshold-scope]

When a condition covers more than 300 entities, reviewing and adjusting thresholds entity by entity becomes impractical. Threshold scope lets you group entities by telemetry attributes so you can manage thresholds at the group level.

To use threshold scope:

1. On the condition page, select up to three entity attributes. Entities sharing the same values for those attributes are grouped together.
2. Review and adjust the threshold for each group.
3. To inspect which entities belong to a group, click the entity count for that group.

<Callout variant="tip">
Thresholding supports a maximum of 5,000 entities and may take a few minutes to complete.
</Callout>
