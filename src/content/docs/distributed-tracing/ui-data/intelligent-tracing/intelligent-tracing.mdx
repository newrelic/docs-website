When an alert fires, the real work begins: finding exactly where the problem is and understanding why it's happening. Manually filtering trace lists, rebuilding queries from scratch, and chasing fragmented data turns what should be a quick fix into an expensive, high-stress investigation that directly inflates Mean Time to Recovery (MTTR).

Intelligent Tracing automates this investigation. It answers two questions — **where** is the problem, and **why** is it happening — through two interconnected capabilities that work together to take you from a spike on a chart to a root-cause finding in clicks.

## How they work together [#workflow]

The two capabilities are designed to be used in sequence during an active incident:

1. **Start from a spike**: Click a time window on any APM latency or error chart. Intelligent Exemplar preserves your context and opens a landing page with ranked anomalous traces and detected patterns.
2. **Navigate to the failing span**: Select a trace from the list. Intelligent Exemplar takes you directly to the anomalous span — not the root span, not the slowest span, but the one deviating from baseline behavior.
3. **Identify the root cause**: From the landing page, run Attribute Correlation Analysis. It compares your anomalous traces against healthy ones and surfaces the specific attribute responsible, with an AI-generated plain-language explanation.

## Requirements [#requirements]

- [Distributed tracing](/docs/distributed-tracing/concepts/introduction-distributed-tracing/) must be enabled for your services.
- Services must be instrumented with a New Relic APM agent.
- New Relic AI must be enabled on your account to generate AI summaries in Attribute Correlation Analysis. AI summary generation incurs additional costs based on your New Relic AI usage.

## Get started [#get-started]

- See [Intelligent Exemplar](/docs/distributed-tracing/ui-data/intelligent-tracing/intelligent-exemplar/) to learn how to surface the right traces and navigate to the failing span.
- See [Attribute Correlation Analysis](/docs/distributed-tracing/ui-data/intelligent-tracing/attribute-correlation-analysis/) to learn how to identify the attributes driving an anomaly.
