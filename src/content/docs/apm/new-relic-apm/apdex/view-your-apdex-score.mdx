Your [Apdex score](/docs/apm/new-relic-apm/apdex/apdex-measuring-user-satisfaction) measures users' satisfaction with the response time of your web applications and services. The [dissatisfaction score](/docs/apm/new-relic-apm/apdex/apdex-measuring-user-satisfaction#dissatisfaction) is the difference between a perfect Apdex score (1.0) and your app's [`apdex_t`](/docs/accounts-partnerships/education/getting-started-new-relic/glossary#apdex_t) ("tolerating") score based on [your Apdex settings](/docs/apm/new-relic-apm/apdex/change-your-apdex-settings).

To help identify and troubleshoot Apdex level changes that indicate poor customer experiences with your site, use any of these resources:

* New Relic's user interface (explained in this document)
* New Relic [REST API](/docs/apis/rest-api-v2/application-examples-v2/getting-apdex-data-apps-or-browsers-v2)
* [Alert notifications](/docs/alerts/new-relic-alerts/configuring-alert-policies/define-thresholds-trigger-alert)

<Callout variant="tip">
  Apdex applies only to web apps or services. The **Apdex** chart in the UI appears blank for [non-web transactions](/docs/apm/transactions/intro-transactions/monitor-background-processes-other-non-web-transactions). To get a high-level overview of all your applications and services, use the [New Relic Explorer](/docs/new-relic-one/use-new-relic-one/ui-data/new-relic-one-entity-explorer).
</Callout>

## View Apdex score in APM [#view_apdex]

Apdex dissatisfaction levels of `Tolerating` ([`apdex_t`](/docs/accounts-partnerships/education/getting-started-new-relic/glossary#apdex_t)) and `Frustrated` ([`apdex_f`](/docs/accounts-partnerships/education/getting-started-new-relic/glossary#apdex_f)) indicate how slow site performance contributes to poor customer experiences in your app. For example:

* 1.0: All responses are satisfactory.
* [Tolerating responses](/docs/apm/new-relic-apm/apdex/apdex-measuring-user-satisfaction#levels) half satisfy a user. For example, if all responses are `Tolerating,` then the Apdex score will be 0.50.
* 0.0: None of the responses are satisfactory.

To view the Apdex score for your web apps or services:

1. Go to **[one.newrelic.com](https://one.newrelic.com) > (select an app) > Summary**.
2. From the [APM **Summary** page](/docs/apm/applications-menu/monitoring/apm-overview-page), review the **Apdex score** chart.

## View transactions with highest Apdex dissatisfaction [#apdex-dissat]

[Transactions](/docs/apm/transactions/intro-transactions/transactions-new-relic-apm) at the top of APM's [**Transactions** page](/docs/apm/applications-menu/monitoring/transactions-page) often are good candidates for performance tuning or fixing errors. To view transactions with the highest Apdex dissatisfaction percentage:

1. Go to **[one.newrelic.com](https://one.newrelic.com/) > APM > (select an app) > Transactions > See transactions table**.
2. Sort **Apdex** to find the most dissatisfying.
3. Specific web transaction: To view details about a specific transaction, select its row.

By definition, the **All transactions** row always contributes 100% of the app's total dissatisfaction, even if no responses are dissatisfying (100% of zero is zero). In this situation, 100% does not mean that all of your transactions are dissatisfying. The sum of all the other values in this column is 100%.

To focus on Apdex levels for specific transactions, you can also:

* Configure [transaction traces](/docs/apm/transactions/transaction-traces/configure-transaction-traces) to capture [`apdex_f`](/docs/accounts-partnerships/education/getting-started-new-relic/glossary#apdex_f), which is four times your app server's [`apdex_t`](/docs/accounts-partnerships/education/getting-started-new-relic/glossary#apdex_t).
* Create [key transactions](/docs/apm/transactions/key-transactions/key-transactions-tracking-important-transactions-or-events) to track changes in Apdex values for specific transactions that are important to your business, such as signups, purchase confirmations, searches, site logins, etc.

## View Apdex score in browser [#browser]

Use [browser monitoring](/docs/browser/new-relic-browser/getting-started/new-relic-browser) to:

* Set Apdex levels for [browser monitoring](/docs/browser/new-relic-browser/installation-configuration/browser-settings-ui-options-browser-monitoring#apdex).
* Review Apdex levels from the perspective of [real-user browser performance](/docs/browser/new-relic-browser/getting-started/browser-overview-website-performance-glance) of your app.
* Track browser performance levels for [selected countries](/docs/browser/new-relic-browser/browser-pro-features/filterable-geography-webpage-metrics-location) you want to monitor.
