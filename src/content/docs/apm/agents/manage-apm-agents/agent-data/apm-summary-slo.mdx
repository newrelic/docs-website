Service level objectives (SLOs) translate technical metrics into commitments: your service will respond within X milliseconds for Y% of requests, or return errors less than Z% of the time. The APM summary page surfaces SLO compliance at a glance — so you can check weekly health, catch budget burn before it becomes a breach, and have numbers ready for stakeholder conversations.

To open the APM summary page, go to <DNT>**[one.newrelic.com > All capabilities](https://one.newrelic.com/all-capabilities) > APM & services**</DNT> and select your application.

<Callout variant="important">
  The **Service levels** tile only populates if you've configured service levels for this entity. If the tile is empty, see [Introduction to service levels](/docs/service-level-management/intro-slm/) to get started. The rest of this page assumes service levels are already configured.
</Callout>

## Read the service levels tile [#service-levels-tile]

The **Service levels** tile on the summary page gives you an instant health check without opening any other pages.

| Tile state | What it means | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| **On track** | Budget is burning at or below the expected rate for the current period | No action needed — review during your normal weekly cadence |
| **At risk** | Budget is burning faster than expected; you could breach the SLO before the period ends | Investigate now — check which SLI is burning and correlate with recent deployments or traffic changes |
| **Non-compliant** | SLO already breached for the current period | Escalate; treat as an active incident if not already being handled |

Click the tile to open the full **Service levels** page for detailed burn rate charts, SLI breakdowns, and remaining budget.

## Run a weekly SLO health check [#weekly-check]

A weekly SLO review takes less than five minutes using the APM summary page as your starting point.

**What to do:**
1. Open the APM summary page for your service.
2. Set the time picker to **Last 7 days**.
3. Check the **Service levels** tile state. If it shows **On track**, your SLO is healthy for the week.
4. Click the tile to open the full service levels page. Review:
   - **Error budget remaining:** How much budget is left for the rest of the period? If you're three weeks into a monthly SLO and 80% of the budget is already consumed, you have a problem even if the tile shows "on track."
   - **Burn rate:** Is the budget being consumed at a steady rate, or were there spikes? Spikes correspond to incidents — verify each one is documented.
5. Cross-reference with the **Errors** chart on the summary page. Confirm that error budget consumption aligns with visible error spikes — if the budget is burning but no errors are visible, your SLI definition may need review.

## Correlate SLO health with service events [#correlate-events]

Budget burn doesn't happen uniformly. Correlate consumption spikes with service events to understand what's driving your SLO health.

**What to do:**
1. On the service levels page (reached by clicking the tile), look for periods where the burn rate accelerated.
2. Return to the APM summary page and set the time picker to the period of high burn.
3. Check for:
   - **Deployment markers** (gray pins on the charts) — a deployment at the start of a burn spike is a strong signal
   - **Error rate spikes** in the Errors chart — these directly consume error-based SLO budget
   - **Apdex drops** — these consume latency-based SLO budget
4. Document each correlation: date, event (deployment/incident/traffic spike), budget consumed. This becomes your SLO narrative for stakeholders.

## Prepare a stakeholder report [#stakeholder-report]

When reporting service health to non-technical stakeholders, the summary page gives you the numbers you need without requiring them to interpret charts.

**Key metrics to pull for a service health report:**

| Metric | Where to find it | What it communicates |
|---|---|---|
| SLO compliance status | Service levels tile | Simple pass/fail health signal |
| Error budget remaining | Service levels detail page | How much room is left before breaching the SLO |
| Apdex over the period | Apdex chart (set time picker to reporting period) | User satisfaction trend |
| Incidents during period | Issues tile — click to see full list | Count and severity of disruptions |
| Deployments during period | Last deployment tile | Pace of change and any associated regressions |

<Callout variant="tip">
  For recurring reporting, consider building a New Relic dashboard that surfaces these metrics automatically. See [Introduction to dashboards](/docs/query-your-data/explore-query-data/dashboards/introduction-dashboards/) to get started.
</Callout>

## Catch budget burn early [#early-warning]

Waiting for the service levels tile to turn red means you've already lost budget. Alert on burn rate instead — get notified when budget consumption accelerates before the SLO is at risk.

**What to do:**
1. Go to <DNT>**[one.newrelic.com > Service levels](https://one.newrelic.com/service-levels)**</DNT>.
2. Select your service level and click **Alert conditions**.
3. Configure a burn rate alert: a common setting is to alert when the 1-hour burn rate exceeds 14x (meaning you'd exhaust a 30-day budget in roughly 2 days at that rate).

See [Alert on service levels](/docs/service-level-management/alerts-slm/) for detailed configuration guidance.
