Once you've installed a New Relic <InlinePopover type="apm"/> agent, the APM summary page is your starting point for understanding your service. It gives you a real-time view of your service's health — response time, throughput, error rates, and how your service connects to the rest of your stack.

To open it, go to <DNT>**[one.newrelic.com > All capabilities](https://one.newrelic.com/all-capabilities) > APM & services**</DNT> and select your application.

The page is organized into three sections from top to bottom:

1. **Summary tiles** — status indicators for alerts, deployments, service levels, and vulnerabilities
2. **Key metrics charts** — continuous performance data for Apdex, transactions, throughput, and errors
3. **Context sections** — logs, distributed tracing, and infrastructure connected to your service

  title="apm summary page"
  alt="A screenshot depicting the APM summary page."
  src="/images/apm_screenshot-full_apm-complete-summary-page-.webp"
/>

<Callout variant="important">
  If you're not seeing data in the charts below, your agent may need additional configuration. Some metrics — such as distributed tracing, logs in context, or infrastructure data — require specific settings to be enabled in your agent config file. See [Configure your APM agent](/docs/apm/agents/manage-apm-agents/configuration/server-side-agent-configuration/) to verify your setup.
</Callout>

<Callout variant="tip">
  On a fresh install, some summary tiles will appear empty. Each tile requires its own feature to be configured before it shows data. Click **Set up now** on any empty tile to get started.
</Callout>

## Summary tiles [#summary-tiles]

The summary tiles at the top of the page give you an at-a-glance status of key areas: open issues, recent deployments, service levels, and security vulnerabilities.

<img
  title="apm summary tiles"
  alt="A screenshot depicting the apm summary tiles"
  src="/images/apm_screenshot-crop_apm-summary-tiles.webp"
/>

<CollapserGroup>
  <Collapser
    id="issues"
    title="Issues"
  >
    The Issues tile shows any open alert incidents affecting your service. It only populates if you have [alert conditions](/docs/alerts-applied-intelligence/new-relic-alerts/get-started/your-first-nrql-condition/) configured for this entity.

    Each issue is made up of one or more alert events. Click the tile to see details about each issue, the alert events it contains, and any related entities.

    <img
      title="issues summary tile"
      alt="A screenshot depicting the issues summary tile"
      src="/images/apm_screenshot-crop_issues-summary-tile.webp"
    />
  </Collapser>

  <Collapser
    id="last-deployment"
    title="Last deployment"
  >
    The Last deployment tile shows how your most recent deployment affected your service's error rate and response time. It only appears if you've set up [deployment markers](/docs/apm/apm-ui-pages/events/record-deployments/).

    Starting 15 minutes after a deployment, this tile displays a comparison between data collected before and after. If you deployed 45 minutes ago, it compares the 45 minutes before and after. For the first three hours, the tile compares elapsed time since deployment against the same duration before it. After three hours, it uses a standard 3-hour window.

    For a custom time range, click the tile to go to the main deployments page and use the time picker there.

    <img
      title="deployment summary tile"
      alt="A screenshot showing the deployment summary tile"
      src="/images/apm_screenshot-crop_deployment-summary-tile.webp"
    />
  </Collapser>

  <Collapser
    id="service-levels"
    title="Service levels"
  >
    The Service levels tile shows whether your service is meeting its defined performance targets. It only appears if you have [service levels configured](/docs/service-level-management/intro-slm/).

    When a service level's error budget is exceeded, you'll see one of these states:

    * <DNT>**Non-compliant**</DNT> means you've consumed all of the error budget for this period. Be cautious about deploying and plan work to improve your SLs.
    * <DNT>**At risk**</DNT> means your error budget is nearly consumed. Be more cautious for the rest of the period.

    Click the tile to see which entities are non-compliant and review your error budget in detail.

    <img
      title="service levels tile"
      alt="A screenshot depicting the service levels summary tile"
      src="/images/apm_screenshot-crop_apm-service-levels-summary-tile.webp"
    />
  </Collapser>

  <Collapser
    id="vulnerabilities"
    title="Vulnerabilities"
  >
    The Vulnerabilities tile shows known security vulnerabilities detected in your service. It populates automatically when you have [Vulnerability Management](/docs/vulnerability-management/overview/) enabled. Each integrated agent or service — like Java, .NET, or GitHub [Dependabot](https://newrelic.com/instant-observability/github-dependabot) — automatically surfaces known vulnerabilities to New Relic.

    Click the tile to view your vulnerability impact and severity breakdown.

    <img
      title="vulnerabilities summary tile"
      alt="A screenshot depicting the apm vulnerabilities summary tile"
      src="/images/apm_screenshot-crop_apm-vulnerabilities-tile.webp"
    />
  </Collapser>
</CollapserGroup>

## Key metrics [#key-metrics]

Below the summary tiles, the key metrics charts give you a continuous view of your service's performance. Use these charts to establish what "normal" looks like for your service and to spot deviations over time.

<CollapserGroup>
  <Collapser
    id="apdex"
    title="Apdex"
  >
    <img
      title="apdex in APM"
      alt="A screenshot depicting a sample Apdex score in APM"
      src="/images/apm_screenshot-crop_apdex-in-apm.webp"
    />

    Apdex measures overall user satisfaction with your application's response time. It's an industry standard score from 0 to 1 — the closer to 1, the better your app is performing.

    The default satisfactory response time threshold (Apdex T) is 0.5 seconds. You can adjust this under **Settings** to match the expected performance of your specific service.

    Your Apdex score is color-coded as follows:

    * <DNT>**&lt; 0.5 - Red:**</DNT> Your application's performance is beyond critical and needs immediate action.
    * <DNT>**0.5-0.7 - Orange:**</DNT> There are performance issues in your application that need investigation.
    * <DNT>**0.7-0.85 - Yellow:**</DNT> Your application is trending in a negative direction.
    * <DNT>**0.85-0.95 - Blue:**</DNT> This is the ideal Apdex range. Your Apdex T is well-tuned and performance is healthy.
    * <DNT>**> 0.95 - Blue:**</DNT> If your score is consistently above 0.95, your Apdex T may be set too high, meaning you're not getting an accurate read on performance. Consider reducing your Apdex T.

    <Callout variant="tip">
      If you have an Apdex score of 0, it's because a request returned with an error. Every request with an error automatically scores a 0 on Apdex.
    </Callout>

    For more about how to understand your score, see [Apdex: measure user satisfaction](/docs/apm/new-relic-apm/apdex/apdex-measure-user-satisfaction/).
  </Collapser>

  <Collapser
    id="web-transactions"
    title="Web transactions"
  >
    <img
      title="web transactions apm summary"
      alt="A screenshot depicting web transactions in the APM summary page"
      src="/images/apm_screenshot-crop_web-transactions-in-APM.webp"
    />

    The **Web transactions** chart shows response time broken down by what your service is spending time on. It uses three color segments:

    * Blue segment: all transactions
    * Yellow segment: database operations
    * Green segment: external services

    Spikes in the chart indicate transactions that took longer than normal to respond. Use [deployment markers](/docs/apm/apm-ui-pages/events/record-deployments/) to correlate response time changes with specific code releases — deployment markers appear as gray pins on the chart.

    <Callout variant="tip">
      If you're monitoring an asynchronous application, your overall response time (the dark blue line) may be lower than the response times for individual segments. Asynchronous applications process multiple requests simultaneously, so a transaction can "end" while some requests are still open.
    </Callout>
  </Collapser>

  <Collapser
    id="throughput"
    title="Throughput"
  >
    <img
      title="throughput in APM"
      alt="A screenshot depicting throughput in APM."
      src="/images/apm_screenshot-full_throughput-in-apm.webp"
    />

    **Throughput** measures the volume of requests your application is handling, typically in requests per minute (rpm). It helps you understand whether work is evenly distributed across your hosts and containers.

    * **Higher than usual throughput** during a period of degradation may indicate your application is processing more work than it can handle.
    * **Lower than usual throughput** may mean a dependent service that calls your application is broken, or that traffic has dropped unexpectedly.
  </Collapser>

  <Collapser
    id="errors"
    title="Errors"
  >
    <img
      title="errors in APM"
      alt="A screenshot showing errors in APM."
      src="/images/apm_screenshot-full_errors-in-APM.webp"
    />

    The **Errors** chart shows the percentage of transactions that resulted in an error. In APM, errors represent `TransactionError` and `ErrorTrace` events.

    Use the dropdown to switch between error rate and a view of impacted users. For how to track impacted users, see [Errors inbox](/docs/errors-inbox/error-users-impacted/).

    For more about how to manage errors, see [Manage errors](/docs/apm/agents/manage-apm-agents/agent-data/manage-errors-apm-collect-ignore-or-mark-expected/).
  </Collapser>
</CollapserGroup>

## Your service in context [#service-context]

The lower sections of the APM summary page connect your service to the broader system — helping you understand whether issues originate upstream, downstream, or in your own infrastructure.

<CollapserGroup>
  <Collapser
    id="logs"
    title="Logs"
  >
    <img
      title="logs in apm"
      alt="A screenshot showing logs in apm"
      src="/images/apm_screenshot-crop_logs-in-apm.webp"
    />

    The logs chart gives you a summary view of your application's logs through the [logs in context](/docs/logs/logs-context/get-started-logs-context/) feature. Logs are automatically tagged with the entity or service they relate to.

    Select **Log patterns (top 10)** to display groups of logs that share the same structure, making it easier to spot recurring errors or unusual activity. Clicking **Logs** takes you to the full [logs UI](/docs/logs/ui-data/use-logs-ui/).

    For more about how log patterns work, see [Log patterns](/docs/logs/ui-data/find-unusual-logs-log-patterns/).
  </Collapser>

  <Collapser
    id="distributed-tracing"
    title="Distributed tracing"
  >
    <img
      title="distributed tracing in APM"
      alt="A screenshot depicting distributed tracing information on the APM summary page"
      src="/images/apm_screenshot-crop_distributed-tracing-in-the-apm-summary-page.webp"
    />

    The **Distributed tracing insights** chart shows upstream and downstream entities that may be contributing to changes in your service's response time, error rate, or throughput.

    For example, if your response time spikes due to an external call, distributed tracing can surface the downstream entity responsible. Click **View trace** to see the full trace and where the latency is introduced.

    You can learn more in the [distributed tracing insights docs](/docs/distributed-tracing/ui-data/related-trace-entity-signals/).
  </Collapser>

  <Collapser
    id="infrastructure"
    title="Infrastructure"
  >
    <img
      title="Infrastructure summary page"
      alt="A screenshot depicting the infrastructure section of the apm summary page."
      src="/images/apm_screenshot-crop_infrastructure-on-apm-summary-page.webp"
    />

    Scroll down to the **Infrastructure** section to see a table of each host connected to your application, including their response time, throughput, error rate, CPU percentage, and memory percentage.

    Use this section to check whether performance issues are isolated to a specific host or spread across all instances. Learn more about [infrastructure data on the APM summary page](/docs/infrastructure/manage-your-data/data-instrumentation/apm-data-infrastructure-monitoring/).
  </Collapser>
</CollapserGroup>

## Explore more in APM [#explore-more]

The APM summary page is your entry point. Use the left-side navigation to dig deeper into any part of your service:

* **Monitor:** [Transactions](/docs/apm/apm-ui-pages/monitoring/transactions-page-find-specific-performance-problems/), [Databases](/docs/apm/apm-ui-pages/monitoring/databases-page-view-operations-throughput-response-time/), [External services](/docs/apm/apm-ui-pages/monitoring/external-services/external-services-intro/), [Kubernetes](/docs/apm/apm-ui-pages/monitoring/kubernetes-summary-page/)
* **Events:** [Deployments](/docs/apm/apm-ui-pages/events/record-deployments/), [Thread profiler](/docs/apm/apm-ui-pages/events/thread-profiler-tool/)
* **Errors:** [Errors inbox](/docs/errors-inbox/apm-tab/)
* **Security:** [Vulnerability Management](/docs/vulnerability-management/applications/overview/)
