New Relic Autopilot is here, and it changes what an on-call shift feels like. When a service degrades, the clock starts, and every minute of manual investigation is a minute of customer impact. SREs know the drill: pivot across dashboards, scan logs, check recent deploys, hunt down the right runbook, and page the person who saw this last. Autopilot collapses that scramble into a single, guided investigation.

New Relic Autopilot is an AI-powered operational assistant that helps engineering and operations teams investigate incidents, identify probable root causes, explain its reasoning, recommend next actions, and help accelerate resolution. The result is that Autopilot helps lower mean time to resolution and reduces operational toil for the engineers carrying the pager.

Point Autopilot at an alert and it can help:

* Triage and validate alerts against historical baselines and current health, so you can determine whether an event is real or noise.
* Summarize the health of a service or a group of related entities, with links to the underlying telemetry so you can verify every finding.
* Pinpoint regressions, new error patterns, and latency introduced by recent deployments.
* Traverse distributed traces, logs, and infrastructure metrics to isolate the likely cause and blast radius.
* Recommend remediation, such as a deployment rollback, for you to review and approve.

## Built for the way SREs actually work

Autopilot gets sharper the more it knows about your environment. Connect New Relic AI Knowledge to pull your runbooks, retrospectives, and Confluence docs into investigations, so recommendations reflect how your team actually operates and surface the fix that worked last time. Turn on Memories and Autopilot carries your preferences, past resolutions, and environment quirks across sessions, so you can build on your history and expertise.

## Investigate without leaving the conversation

With the Slack integration, mention the New Relic app in any channel to triage an alert, check an entity, or run a multi-turn investigation right in the thread, using your own New Relic permissions. The whole team stays in the loop, and issues get resolved where the work is already happening.
